I got my mbed today!  I haven’t played with it yet, but I hope to use it to make some cool stuff soon.

I really like how it comes with a plastic wallet insert pinout diagram.  They really expect you to carry the mbed around with you in your pocket and just whip it out whenever you need to build something.  

Hehe, I should implement some of the ESE 350 labs on the mbed.  Then, when someone complains to me about how their HC12 isn’t working, I can be all like, “HAHAHA DON’T YOU WISH YOU HAD ONE OF THEEEEEEESE?” And then that’s when I take it out, plug it into the USB port, hit the go button, and BAM:  instant DTMF generator.

I have a few projects in mind for my mbed, but I’m also open to suggestions. :)
